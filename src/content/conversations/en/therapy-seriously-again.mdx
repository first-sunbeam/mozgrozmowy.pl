## Must-Have Checklist for Compatible Therapy

### Model of work and the therapist's position

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Freestyle or pole choreography? (creativity vs. routine)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Freestyle! Frames are for paintings, not neurons." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="A therapist as artist or photocopier? (flexibility vs. thoughtlessness)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Definitely an artist. A photocopier once tried to understand me... and jammed." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Repair service or support with empathy? (diagnosis and correction vs. relationship-building)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Empathy! I already have diagnoses and corrections. Now I'd like someone who won't treat me like a broken blender under warranty." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Social acceptance or warranty service for neurons? (social model vs. medical model of disability)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Acceptance! I'm not here for a reset — I'm a beta version with no intention of becoming final." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Empathy with a social modem, or operating in 'don't know, can't see, doesn't apply' mode? (awareness of social barriers vs. ignoring context)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Empathy with a modem! Otherwise it's like trying to play an RPG without understanding who your character is or what world they live in." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="A shared journey, or riding in the back seat while the therapist takes the wheel? (partnership vs. hierarchy in therapy)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Partnership! I'm the one sitting in this vehicle all the time — let the therapist help with the map, not take over the steering wheel." />

## Discovery vs. censorship

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Discovering your own plot, or censoring symptoms in the final cut? (understanding symptoms vs. reducing them)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Definitely discovering the plot! Symptoms aren't errors — they're trailers telling you that something important is happening inside me." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Behavior: a message in a bottle, or something skipped without reading? (behavior as communication vs. unwanted symptom)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="A message. My behavior doesn't appear for no reason — it's a bottle carrying information, not a random object drifting by." />

## Accessibility and communication

### Contact, pace, and processing style

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Online form or an analog interface with noise in the background? (online accessibility vs. excluding forms of contact)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Online! I have no compatibility with voice calls — text only, zero emotional lag." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="'Slow processing' mode with a pause button, or racing through the session on a timer? (space for processing vs. time pressure)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Longer sessions! My emotion-loading system has a progress bar, not a turbo button." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="A picture, a note, a mind map — or endless talking with no support? (visual materials vs. talking only)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Visual support, please! My auditory memory is like a browser with fifty open tabs — one graphic is like pinning a bookmark." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Your waves, or just one broadcast channel? (adapted communication vs. rigid format)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="If you speak only one language, you won't hear mine. And I have text mode, visual mode, silence... and sometimes memes." />

## Inner world and lived experience

### Masking, burnout, and the cost of functioning

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Autistic burnout as a distinct state, or thrown into the 'depression.zip' folder? (distinguishing autistic burnout from depression)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="It's not 'sadness' — it's a system in emergency mode. Autistic burnout is not a temporary drop in mood, but a total system overload. Don't confuse a restart with a freeze — this is not a case of 'rest and you'll be back to normal.'" />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Freely being yourself, or daily cosplay as a neurotypical citizen? (authenticity vs. masking)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Authenticity, please! Pretending to be 'normal' is like playing a game on the highest difficulty setting — with no breaks and no save option." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Sensory-system overload, or just logging thoughts and emotions? (working with body and senses vs. pure cognition)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="I need the full picture! Sometimes the problem isn't thoughts at all, but the fact that a clothing tag is pushing me to the edge of endurance." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Through the prism of neurodiversity, or through the filter of 'that's how everyone does it'? (neurodivergent perspective vs. neurotypical norms)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="I need someone who sees the world from my perspective — not through the lens of 'normal,' which smooths everything over until what actually matters disappears." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Avoiding noise, or 'panic for no reason'? (sensory reaction vs. anxiety)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Not everything that looks like anxiety actually is. Sometimes it's just my way of surviving without overheating the whole system." />

### Sensory load, executive functioning, and everyday viability

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Support with planning and executive chaos, or the expectation that I'll sort myself out 'because that's what you're supposed to do'? (executive support vs. demanding independence)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Help is welcome! My executive functions do not always work on schedule — without support it's like putting together a puzzle in the dark. And yes, Monday got lost again." />

## Therapy process and relationship

### After the session, transparency, and relational safety

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Homework as a personalized quest, or total logout after the session? (tailored tasks vs. lack of integration between sessions)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Give me tasks, but not on 'hardcore mode.' Something short, specific — and ideally with a checkpoint and a reward at the end." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Transparency like an open window, or therapy behind tinted glass? (therapist transparency vs. lack of clarity and information)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Transparency, please! How am I supposed to trust you if I don't know what's happening on the other side of the desk? This isn't a psychological thriller — it's supposed to be collaboration." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Actions that respect the system's history, or a factory reset? (taking earlier wounds into account vs. minimizing history)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="My previous experiences matter. If you ignore them, it's like updating a system without checking the error logs first." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Asking about pronouns, or playing 'guess who I am'? (respecting identity vs. assumptions)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="This isn't a quiz. The question costs nothing, but it may make me feel genuinely seen." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Looking in the mirror, or hiding behind a curtain of infallibility? (therapist honesty vs. avoiding mistakes)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Honesty! No one is perfect, but honesty is like a security update — it protects the relationship from freezing." />

### Language, adaptation, and up-to-date knowledge

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Plain language, or 'hard mode: psychoterminology'? (understandable language vs. specialist jargon)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Less Latin, more meaning. My processor works better without having to decode every conversation like a logic puzzle." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Individual settings, or 'one format for everyone'? (adaptation to information-processing style)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="I'm not a .doc file — my data has to be read in a mode compatible with the autistic extension." />

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Learning as you go, or 'I finished my degree, that's enough'? (updating knowledge vs. stagnation)" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="If you've been offline for ten years, don't be surprised that your approach no longer works on my system." />
