## #BrainInTherapy

🧠💬 **"Brain in therapy"** - that is what happens inside when the therapist asks a question…

### Internal process: What happens when questions are asked?

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Hey, Brain… can we talk?" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Because when on therapy these questions fall:" />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="'How are you today?' 'What did you come with?' 'What do you want to talk about today?' 'What do you feel in your body?' 'What tells you that this is exactly how it is?' 'Can you allow someone to see this?'… and many others…" />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="…I often answer: 'I don't know', 'I guess so', 'Honestly nothing specific'." />

### Security system activates immediately

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="And You, Brain:" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="you activate security system," />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="analyze tone, expression and milliseconds of silence," />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="scan all previous experiences," />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="and before I say anything, You have already counted everything." />

### Time needed for answer

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Yes. Sometimes to answer you need warmth, empathy… and more than socially comfortable 30 seconds of silence. Often processing time can be one minute, five minutes or… appear only after the session." />

## Scene 1: Soft Question, Hard Landing

### Question without vector

<DialogLine speaker="therapist" text="(T) 'What makes you see it exactly like this?'" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="(internal monologue): Wait, what… what exactly do I 'see'? This moment? That situation from five years ago? My reaction now? Or something more general?" />

### Causality model – which one?

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="'What makes', so I have to indicate cause. But in which causality model? Psychological, biographical, social?" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="This question has no vector. It's not clear if it's about feeling, data, belief, or general narrative." />

### "You see" – what does that actually mean?

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="And this 'you see'… Does that mean: what I think, what I know, what I feel, what I imagine? After all I see things differently than others. Specifically, literally, in structures." />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="And I have to answer spontaneously, though I don't know what it's about. I feel this question has intention of being warm and inviting, but in my system there's no way to process it." />

### Answer and interpretation

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="(K) 'I don't know. I just feel it like this.'" />
<DialogLine speaker="therapist" text="(T) 'That's important. Thank you for sharing this with me.'" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="(internal monologue): I don't know what I shared. But it passed. System: frozen, interpretation: uncertain, internal alignment: low." />

### Comment

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="🔍 **Comment:** For neurotypical person this question may sound like neutral, warm invitation to reflection. For autistic person – like message without clear parameters that need to be independently broken down into parts before any answer falls." />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And that's exactly why it's worth adapting language to how brain works. Not to simplify. To enable access." />

## Scene 2: Soft Question, Hard Landing – Understandable Version

### Question with structure

<DialogLine speaker="therapist" text="(T) 'I'd like to better understand how you came to this conclusion. Can you tell what specifically led you to it?'" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="(internal monologue): Phew. Good, this question has structure. I know what it's about ('this conclusion'), I know what you expect from me (causes), and I know I can reach for data from past." />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="I can fly through folder 'experiences', 'others' reactions' and 'situation analysis'. I don't have to guess tone or intention. There's space for pause and no empathy test." />

### Finding sense

<DialogLine speaker="me" text="(K) 'I think because a few times I met with such reaction. And since then I read it faster, before someone says something.'" />
<DialogLine speaker="therapist" text="(T) 'That's very important information. Thank you for showing me this.'" />

### Internal alignment: high!

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="(internal monologue): Sense found. Answer was possible. System didn't need to defend itself. Internal alignment: high." />

## Summary

Adapting language to autistic thinking isn't simplification – it's **enabling access**. When question has structure, vector and clear goal, brain can answer without activating defensive system. This isn't about ability – it's about communication compatibility.
