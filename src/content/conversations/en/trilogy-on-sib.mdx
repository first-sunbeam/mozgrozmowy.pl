A dialogue based on the study's conclusions and their implications for practice, not a literal summary of the article.

## Part I — What is this study about?

<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="The study examines self-injurious behaviours (SIB) in 594 children and adolescents on the autism spectrum."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Wait. What exactly is SIB?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="These are behaviours in which a child disrupts their own physical integrity: head-banging, hand-biting, scratching until wounds form. Their function is not destruction, but regulation. Most often they serve as an emergency regulatory mechanism when the nervous system can find no other route."
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="me"
	text="Okay. So the thing we heard in class: the 'more severe' the autism, the more SIB. The study probably doesn't change that?"
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="No. According to the study, the severity of autistic traits was not a predictor of SIB."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Oh?... Maybe then intellectual level?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Also no significant connection. Cognitive functioning did not explain SIB severity."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="I give up. So what actually mattered?" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain">
	<p>Most strongly associated with SIB severity were:</p>
	<ul>
		<li>sensory avoidance,</li>
		<li>sleep problems,</li>
		<li>reduced psychological wellbeing of the mother,</li>
		<li>lower educational level of the father.</li>
	</ul>
</DialogLine>
<DialogLine
	speaker="me"
	text="And I already see how someone will reduce this to 'parental blame'... though it's not about blame, but conditions and burdens."
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Yes. These are elements of the system in which the child functions, not an assessment of people. The study shows where burden accumulates, not 'who is at fault'."
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="me"
	text="The article also mentions that the model explains 'about 15% of variance'. What does that mean?"
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="It means that this set of factors explains part of the differences in SIB severity between children, but definitely not all of them. The remaining 85% is complex reality: biology, experience, pain, illness, environmental change, and the organism's current state."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="So that's little or a lot?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="In studies on behaviour, this is typical, valuable result. It's a risk map, not an algorithm predicting an individual child's fate."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Summarize it for me? In one sentence?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="SIB is a regulatory and systemic phenomenon, not a characteristic of autism."
/>

## Part II — What does the brain want in therapy?

<DialogLine
	speaker="me"
	text="Since this is not 'bad behaviour' but an overloaded regulation system, I'll ask directly: what do you actually expect from therapy?"
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Above all, I'd like resistance not to be confused with lack of willingness. Most often it means current demands exceed my resources."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="So the key is cost?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Yes. Every action should first be evaluated for energetic burden, and only then for conceptual appeal. If something overwhelms me, it doesn't become a strategy, it becomes another problem."
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="me"
	text="What about therapeutic goals? Every therapy starts with them, right?"
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Yes. And then I often go blank and grab the first available thought. Goals only make sense after basic stability has been restored. When I am sleep-deprived, sensory-overloaded, or under long-term strain, every goal feels like pressure."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="So sleep really makes a difference?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="A huge difference. Sleep is a fundamental regulatory intervention, not just the background of daily life. Without it, the overload threshold drops dramatically."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And sensory aspects?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="First reduce stimulus load, then expect learning. In constant escape mode I don't have space for new skills."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Motivation?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="I don't need additional motivation. I need functional accessibility. When something is within my energetic reach, I do it without pressure."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And the system: school, procedures?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Let it stop producing regulatory debt that later returns as behaviour — because it does not disappear. It simply shows up later, usually at home after school, not because 'it is safe,' but because the system has nothing left to regulate from."
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="me"
	text="In one sentence, what kind of therapy is okay for you?"
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="One that first reduces life cost, and only then looks at what happens with behaviour."
/>

## Part III — What the brain does NOT want in therapy

<DialogLine
	speaker="me"
	text="Since we already know what helps, I'll ask directly: what do you definitely NOT want in therapy?"
/>
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="I don't want therapy to add burden and then interpret my reaction as a problem."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Specifically?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="I don't want starting with demands, without checking if I have resources for them."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="So goals before regulation?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="That's reversed order. Regulation isn't a means to an end, it is the end itself, and only on that foundation can we build what comes next."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="What else increases cost?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Ignoring sleep. With chronic sleep deprivation every subsequent intervention just deepens chaos."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And sensory environment?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="I do not want 'learning to function' in conditions that are unbearable for me. Reducing stimuli is a condition, not a privilege..."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="Motivating during crisis?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Useless. At zero accessibility no reward system works."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="So pressure, consequences, labels?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="I don't want language that judges instead of explains. Moralizing closes dialogue and increases cost."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And 'consequences'?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="Consequences that don't account for my state escalate rather than teach."
/>
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="In one sentence?" />
<DialogLine
	speaker="brain"
	text="I don't want therapy that treats regulation as an add-on and behaviour as the main problem."
/>
