## The scream. Every parent has a moment of “it starts”

For me, it is a fragment from Apollo 13 — that mission in which nobody screamed even though they were running out of oxygen (full control, of course; a known procedure: oxygen runs out every day).

Technical brain, technical escape into thought. Instead of a scream, I hear: "Houston, we've had a problem." A parent of two children with a nervous system that has been operating in emergency silence mode for a long time (the alarms were turned off long ago — energy economy, not heroism).

## A generation of awareness without resources

I am part of a generation of “awareness” without resources. An autistic parent who knows:
- what my parents did not know,
- that meltdown is not rebellion,
- that shutdown is not offense,
- that this is biology, tension, neurochemistry, serotonin, dopamine, and too much sound,
- and that traditional parenting methods belong in the attic (right next to the boxes of “good advice”).

## You have awareness — and that is exactly why

- you have no strength,
- every thought has a cost,
- you watch the same world grind your children down,
- you see it in HD.

It is the same world, the same good advice, the same manuals written in a language I cannot stand. The difference is you. You have awareness that sounds like a promise for your children, but to you it sounds like a verdict — you no longer have any strength left.

The world says: work on yourself, breathe, be flexible. The only thing flexible in me is my pain threshold. Help comes in normative language, in a translation that exhausts me all over again. For reasons unknown, there are no workshops and no support for autistic parents. “I survived into adulthood” means: adaptation successfully completed. So now, according to the logic of the world, a neurotypical interpretation of family dynamics is supposed to help us. And if that fails, I should be reminded of a training lesson called “the norm.” For me, that is just another energy drain.

## Trying to explain my world

So I try to explain my world:
- that this is not about emotions but about nervous-system energy,
- that this is not resistance but physiology,
- that this is not control but a system trying not to sink.

And then the world looks at me as if I were talking about quantum physics rather than about life.

> "Good autistic parenting is not emotional perfection but honest energy management." (Parenting When You're Autistic — Pooky Knightsmith)

## Parenting in an autistic key

**Autistic parenting is not about boundaries. It is about managing the sensory burden of two (or more) nervous systems in one home.**

For those who are curious: this is exactly what autistic parenting is. Signal. Reception. Analysis. A cold calmness to the world — regulatory calm to me.
