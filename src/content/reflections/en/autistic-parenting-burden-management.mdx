## The Scream. Every Parent Has Their Moment of "It Starts"

I have a fragment from Apollo 13, that mission where no one screamed, even though they were running out of oxygen (full control, you know the procedure: oxygen runs out daily 🫁).

Technical brain, technical escape in thought. Instead of screaming I hear: "Houston, we've had a problem" A parent with two children and a nervous system that has long been operating in emergency silence mode (alarms long turned off - energy economy, not heroism 🔋).

## Generation of Awareness Without Resources

I am a generation of "awareness" without resources. An autistic parent who knows:
- what my parents didn't know
- that meltdown isn't rebellion
- that shutdown isn't offense
- that it's biology, tension, neurochemistry, serotonin, dopamine, and too much sound,
- that there's room on the attic for traditional parenting methods (right next to boxes of "good advice").

## You Have Awareness And That's Why:

- you have no strength
- every thought costs energy
- you watch as this same world overwhelms your children
- you see it in HD version (I don't recommend the upgrade💻).

It's the same world, the same good advice, the same manuals written in a language I can't stand. What's different is You💛. You have awareness that sounds like a promise for your children, but to you it sounds like a verdict - you no longer have strength.

The world says: work on yourself, breathe, be flexible, and I only have a flexible pain threshold. I receive help in normative language, in translation that exhausts me again. For reasons not fully known there are no workshops and support for autistic parents. "I survived to adulthood" (📍you know) means: adaptation completed successfully. Now then, according to the world's logic, neurotypical interpretation of family dynamics will help us. In case of failure, I should be reminded of a training lesson (called norm). For me this is another energy drain (🔋but calmly, at least I know what it's called).

## Trying to Explain My World

So I try to explain my world:
- that it's not emotions but nervous system energy,
- that it's not resistance but physiology,
- that it's not control but a system trying not to sink.
And then the world looks at me as if I were talking about quantum physics, not life.

> 💬 "Good parenting in autistic version is not emotional perfection, but honest energy management." (Parenting When You're Autistic - Pooky Knightsmith)

## Parenting in Autistic Version

💛 **Parenting in autistic version isn't about boundaries. It's managing sensory burden of two (or more) nervous systems in one home.**

🧩 For the curious: this is exactly parenting in autistic version. Communication. Reception. Analysis. Cold calmness (for the world) - for me regulatory calmness.
