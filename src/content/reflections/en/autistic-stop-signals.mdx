## Conversation with the Brain: Four Faces of Crisis – and None Is Laziness

*(Evening. You're sitting at a table, tea in front of you. Beside you – the Brain. It pulls out a paper and pen. Draws four red STOP signs one under another.)*

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Look. This is your path to shutdown. Four signals I send, but you always keep going." />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="But I don't see these signs." />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Because you've learned to call them ‘laziness’ or ‘overreaction’. But they're warnings. Look." />

*(Draws the first sign.)*

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Burnout – fog in your head, migraine, heightened sensitivity to light and noise. A regular light bulb suddenly stabs like a spotlight, background conversation sounds like a pneumatic drill. Plus working memory disappears, simple things take three times longer. This is my first STOP. Your environment says: ‘we're all tired’, but you…" />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And I push through anyway and tell myself ‘it's just fatigue, I'll manage’, because I believe that's what you should do." />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="But it's not ordinary fatigue. It's system burnout." />

*(Draws the second sign.)*

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Inertia – I stop you. You look at a cup, look at dishes, but your body doesn't react. Start and stop are blocked. This is an emergency handbrake. People see this and say: ‘it's just procrastination, pull yourself together’, but you…" />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="I tell myself: ‘I'm lazy, I have to force myself’." />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="But it's protection. It's not laziness. It's a signal that the tank is empty." />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="So I force myself, already running on fumes?" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Exactly. And then we move to the next sign." />

*(Draws the third sign, larger, with an exclamation mark.)*

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Meltdown – fuses blow. It looks like an explosion, but only inside you – no one outside sees it because you're masking. Inside: thought loops that can't be stopped – exactly how a hairdryer engine works, how many layers asphalt has, in what order German chancellors were… and when facts run out, I give you analysis of human behavior with no answers left. The system overheats and crashes. When you tell others what you're thinking, you hear: ‘You're overreacting, dramatizing’, but you…" />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And then I minimize: ‘it's just thoughts, I'm exaggerating, I should ignore this’." />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="But it's not exaggeration or hysteria. It's a fire alarm in the nervous system." />

*(Draws the last sign, huge, surrounded by shadow.)*

<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Shutdown – I turn everything off. I slow down speech, time and reactions, sometimes extinguish them completely. From outside it looks like you're sleeping or offended. People ask: ‘Why aren't you saying anything? What's happening?’. And it is ‘nothing’ – and this isn't indifference. It's hibernation. I withdraw you from reality to save remaining energy, but you..." />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And then I disappear. Don't reply, don't log into work, as if someone cut all cables – ‘disconnected on full’." />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="So every STOP is a warning?" />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Exactly. Burnout, inertia, meltdown – these are distress signals. The earlier you stop, the less it hurts at the end." />
<DialogLine speaker="me" text="And that silence at the end? I feel relief in it." />
<DialogLine speaker="brain" text="Because relief appears when the alarm quiets down. But it's like relief after fainting – you feel nothing because I cut the power. This isn't rest, this is escape. True silence exists – you call it safe emptiness. You can enter it consciously before the system itself kicks me out." />

*(The Brain puts down the pen. On the paper four STOP signs. You look and know: this isn't laziness. It's a map.)*
