## Cognitive Structural Logbook. Off-channel slips

### How do I communicate?

🧠 **My communication style is based on analysis, structure, delayed response.**

For me this means: I'm present, listening, thinking, wanting to answer honestly and precisely.

### How is it received?

❄️ "Cold", because I don't react emotionally immediately
🧊 "Distant", because before answering I need to process
🔩 "Rigid", because I talk about emotions like data
💨 "Emotionally absent", because I don't send those signals which for others are "presence"

### But I am present. Just differently.

And that's exactly why this logbook was created, to show how conversations that make sense to me... in others' perception sound like mistake.

---

## Logbook: Conversations That Don't Resonate

### NT: "Let's talk about our relationship."
**Me:** "I understand you want data. Here's the data."

### NT: "I feel like you're judging me."
**Me:** "I'm not judging. I'm analyzing. Judgment requires valuation. I do functional analysis."

### NT: "It's not about logic, it's about emotions."
**Me:** "Don't emotions follow the logic of nervous system operation? Because if not, then we probably don't have a common channel."

### NT: "Don't complicate it."
**Me:** "I'm not complicating. It just has more than two layers."

### NT: "That was just a question."
**Me:** "There is no such thing as just a question. A question contains assumptions, reference frames and expected response."

### NT: "Why do you analyze like this?"
**Me:** "Because analysis is my form of contact. Tenderness through logic."

### NT: "Just trust me."
**Me:** "But I don't have data that would justify it."

### NT: "You just need to feel what's right."
**Me:** "For me 'right' = coherent. I need to check, not feel."

### NT: "That probably didn't matter so much?"
**Me:** "But it was inconsistent. So I got stuck. And now I can't go further."

### NT: "You're analyzing again!"
**Me:** "Yes. Because that's how I try to grasp reality."

### NT: "I feel this with my whole self."
**Me:** "I process this with delay and in parts."

## Logbook: Therapist vs Me

### Therapist: "Let's see what you feel when you say that."
**Me:** "I feel that you're observing me and looking for reaction, and I'm tensing up because I don't know what you expect from me."

### Therapist: "This sounds like you got lost in it."
**Me:** "No. I didn't get lost. I didn't receive a map."

### Therapist: "This isn't a test, just conversation."
**Me:** "But I can't turn off cognitive security testing function."

### Therapist: "It seems to me that you're afraid of my reaction."
**Me:** "It seems to you... so this isn't a question. Should I do something about it? Is it judgment, assumption, test or hint?"

### Therapist: "I have impression that you're overwhelmed now."
**Me:** "But does your impression have priority over my state now? And how am I supposed to know if it's guess or fact?"

## Logbook: Family and Friends

### Parent: "Why can't you just adapt?"
**Me:** "Because I don't know what. There's no clear pattern."

### Colleague: "That was just a joke."
**Me:** "I didn't recognize it. It had structure of threat."

### Teacher: "Why aren't you smiling?"
**Me:** "Because nobody gave me rule that now it's appropriate."

### Parent: "You're exaggerating. Nobody said anything bad."
**Me:** "It's not about words. It's about what they meant."

### Colleague: "You don't play, so you don't like us."
**Me:** "Not liking ≠ inability to play in your format."

### Partner: "I can't argue with you like a human being."
**Me:** "Because first I need to understand what it's about. And then maybe we'll argue if I decide it's worth it."

## Summary: Double Empathy Problem

This isn't about lack of empathy. It's **difference in processing and communication styles**.

- ✅ For me: analysis = form of contact
- ✅ For others: immediate emotional response = presence
- ✅ I need time to process
- ✅ They expect signals I don't send
- ✅ I see inconsistencies as blockage for further conversation
- ✅ They see this as "too much analyzing"

### Key Difference

**For me:** presence = listening + analysis + honest answer  
**For others:** presence = immediate emotional response + nonverbal signals

This isn't lack of empathy. It's **another form of empathy** that requires time, structure and clarity.
